Mt. Moriah



A cemetary to remember: a cemetary that remembers


Mt. Moriah Cemetery is a historic cemetery located in Philadelphia, PA. Established in 1855, it is one of the oldest cemeteries in the city and has a rich history. The cemetery spans over 270 acres and is home to more than 300,000 burials. It is known for its picturesque grounds, beautiful landscaping and the many notable figures that have been laid to rest there.

The cemetery is notable for its Victorian-era architecture and monuments. The cemetery features many grand mausoleums, ornate tombs and stunning statues that are a testament to the craftsmanship and artistry of the time. Visitors can also see a variety of different styles of headstones and markers, from simple grave markers to grand monuments. The cemetery is also home to many notable figures from Philadelphia's history, including politicians, business leaders, and other prominent citizens.

One of the most iconic features of Mt. Moriah Cemetery is the United States Military Memorial, which was dedicated in the early 20th century. The memorial features a large central statue of a soldier standing at attention, surrounded by smaller statues of soldiers representing different branches of the military. The memorial is a fitting tribute to the many veterans who have been laid to rest in the cemetery.

Mt. Moriah Cemetery is also known for its beautiful gardens and landscaping. The cemetery features many different types of trees and plants, including a large collection of rhododendrons and azaleas. The cemetery also boasts a variety of flowers and other plants, including iris, lilies, and roses. The cemetery's gardens are a peaceful and serene place to visit and reflect.

Mt. Moriah Cemetery is an important and historic site in Philadelphia, PA. Visitors can explore the cemetery's rich history, admire the beautiful Victorian-era architecture, and pay their respects to the many notable figures that have been laid to rest there. The cemetery's picturesque grounds, beautiful landscaping and unique features make it a must-see destination for anyone interested in history, architecture, or simply looking for a peaceful place to reflect.
